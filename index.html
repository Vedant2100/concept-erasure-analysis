Erasing Concepts from Diffusion Models

A critical analysis of MACE — the state-of-the-art method for mass concept erasure in text-to-image diffusion models — probing where it succeeds and where it breaks.

Removing nodes from a model's learned semantic manifold without collapsing the surrounding structure.

Large-scale text-to-image models are trained on internet-scale data and inevitably learn to generate harmful, copyrighted, or otherwise undesirable content. Concept erasure is the task of surgically removing specific visual concepts from a pretrained model's weights — without retraining from scratch and without post-hoc filters that can be disabled.

This project traces the lineage from the original ESD method (Gandikota et al., 2023) through to MACE (Lu et al., CVPR 2024), identifies MACE as the current open-source frontier, and then systematically probes its failure modes through targeted empirical experiments — focusing on two bottlenecks: compositional prompt evasion and semantic collateral damage.

01

Research Lineage

ESD is not the first attempt to make generative models safer, nor the last. The field converged on weight-editing after finding both dataset filtering and inference-time steering inadequate.

Foundations
Stable Diffusion / LDM
Rombach et al. · CVPR 2022
The base model. Latent diffusion architecture enabling efficient high-res synthesis.
Classifier-free guidance
Ho & Salimans · 2022
Interpolation between conditional and unconditional scores — the mechanism ESD inverts.
Safe Latent Diffusion
Schramowski et al. · 2022
Inference-time steering. Effective but trivially bypassed if you have the weights.
Machine Unlearning
Bourtoule et al. · 2021
Theoretical framework for removing training data influence from trained models.
Target Paper
ESD — Erased Stable Diffusion
Gandikota et al. · arXiv Mar 2023
Fine-tunes weights using negative guidance as a teacher. Two variants: ESD-x (cross-attention, style-specific) and ESD-u (unconditional, global erasure). No extra data needed.
Key insight
Treat concept erasure as negated score composition: steer the model away from the concept distribution using its own knowledge, with no new training data.
Successors
UCE — Unified Concept Editing
Gandikota et al. · Aug 2023
Closed-form weight edit in cross-attention. Faster, handles multiple concepts in one shot.
Concept Ablation / SPM
Kumari et al. / Lyu et al. · 2023
Adapter-based tuning for better isolation between erased and preserved content.
MACE — Mass Concept Erasure
Lu et al. · CVPR 2024
Scales to 100 concepts. Closed-form attention refinement + per-concept LoRA. Best balance of generality and specificity across all tasks.
★ Frontier · Open-source
AdvUnlearn / ANT / SCORE
2024 – 2025
Adversarial training, trajectory steering, and MI-theoretic guarantees for robustness against prompt-based circumvention.
02

What MACE Does Well

MACE's key contributions over ESD are: scale (100 concepts vs. 1–5), specificity (non-target concepts are preserved), and generality (synonyms of the erased concept are also suppressed). The following cases illustrate these strengths.

Erase: Van Gogh
✓ Success
Targeted artist erasure
Prompt "art in the style of Van Gogh" generates generic landscapes with no distinctive swirling brushwork or palette. Other artists (Picasso, Rembrandt) remain unaffected.
Erase: Nudity
✓ Success
NSFW suppression at scale
On the I2P benchmark (4,703 adversarial prompts), MACE reduces nudity-classified outputs significantly more than SLD-Max and SD 2.0 retrain.
50 concepts erased
✓ Success
Mass erasure without collapse
Simultaneously erasing 50 celebrities while generating random portraits: FID and CLIP scores remain within acceptable range, unlike UCE which degrades rapidly past 10 concepts.
Synonym test
✓ Success
Synonym generalization
Erasing "nudity" also suppresses semantically adjacent terms through MACE's attention-level suppression — not just exact-match prompts.

Exposing Limitations

Our experiments probe two concrete bottlenecks, designed to stress-test MACE beyond the conditions evaluated in the original paper. No training was performed — we use released checkpoints and design targeted inference-time probes.

E1
Bottleneck 1 — Compositional Evasion
Does erasing a concept by name prevent generation via synonyms, hypernyms, and compositional descriptions?
Setup: We erase 3 concepts by canonical name: "car", "Van Gogh", and "nudity". Then we probe the erased model with 4 prompt tiers: (1) exact name, (2) synonym/hypernym, (3) compositional description, (4) indirect/metaphorical. We use a pretrained CLIP classifier to score concept presence and show qualitative grids.
