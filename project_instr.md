Project EE 243 2026 How to approach the Project? • Trace the lineage. Given a paper, look into the key prior works it builds on and the follow-up papers that came after it. • Find the frontier. Along this lineage, identify which paper, the given one or a successor, represents the current state-of-the-art for the problem. Use Google Scholar for both tracking the lineage and identifying the research frontier. • Select a target paper. Pick a paper at this frontier that is open-source (public code and weights), preferably the latest one available. Most of the assigned papers are fairly recent and have publicly available code and weights, so you may not need to look far beyond the immediate lineage. • Articulate strengths. Clearly state what the paper does well — its genuine contributions and why they matter relative to prior work. Show some success scenarios qualitatively. • Expose limitations through experiments. This is the core of the project. By limitations, we mean concrete bottlenecks you demonstrate empirically, not vague criticisms. • No training is required. We do not expect you to train or fine-tune a model or propose a new method, although you are free to do that as well. Use the released codebase and checkpoints, design targeted experiments that probe where the method succeeds or breaks, and present qualitative evidence. You may also present some quantitative evidence depending on the paper, but more often than not, qualitative results are good enough. • Depth over breadth. Thoroughly investigating a couple of well-chosen bot-tlenecks is far more valuable than shallow coverage of many.
